  • Ada met Charles Babbage through their mutual friend, Mary Somerville, which then Babbage invited her to see the prototype of Difference Engine.
  • Charles Babbage recognised her as The Enchantress of Number
  • Charles Dickens (who is a famous writer back then) used to read to her on her deathbed.
  • At the age of 12, Ada conceptualised a flying machine; illustrated plans on constructing a winged flying apparatus before moving on to think about powered flight.
  • Ada Lovelace was a visionary who predicted that computers could do more than just crunching numbers.
  • A computer programming is named after her, in honour of her. Ada programming language (high-order programming language) was developed by U.S. Department of Defence (1979).
